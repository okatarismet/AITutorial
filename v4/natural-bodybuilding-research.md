# Natural Bodybuilding — Best Movements & Plan (Reddit / Science-Based Research)

## The Natural Lifter's Filter (read this first)

The single biggest "enhanced vs natural" difference isn't *which* exercises — it's
**volume and frequency**. Steroid users recover from 25–30 sets per muscle/week and
5–6 day bro-splits hitting each muscle once. **You cannot.** As a natural, the
evidence-based sweet spot is:

- **10–20 sets per muscle per week** (start ~10–12, add over time)
- **Each muscle trained 2× per week** (frequency beats cramming everything into one
  annihilation day — protein synthesis only stays elevated ~48h for naturals)
- **Every working set taken to 0–3 reps from failure** (RIR). Proximity to failure is
  non-negotiable for naturals since you can't brute-force growth with volume.
- **Progressive overload is the actual driver** — not the pump, not soreness, not
  "muscle confusion."

That alone puts you ahead of 90% of people copying enhanced influencers.

---

## The Split — Upper / Lower (4 days)

For a natural, **Upper/Lower run 4×/week** is the best balance of frequency (2×/muscle),
recovery, and volume. PPL 6× also works but is harder to recover from and over-emphasizes
pushing. If you can only do 3 days, run Full Body 3×.

```
Mon: Upper A    Tue: Lower A    Thu: Upper B    Fri: Lower B
```

## The Movements — 2 per body part (highest-ROI picks)

Reps below are the *working set* range. Use **double progression**: pick a rep range,
add reps until you hit the top of the range on all sets, then add weight and drop back
to the bottom.

### BACK
| Movement | Sets × Reps | Why it's a top pick |
|---|---|---|
| **Weighted Pull-up / Lat Pulldown** | 3–4 × 6–10 | Best vertical pull for lat width. Pull-ups if you can, pulldown to push past failure cleanly. |
| **Chest-Supported Row** (machine or DB) | 3–4 × 8–12 | Chest support removes lower-back/cheating — pure mid-back/lat thickness. |

### CHEST
| Movement | Sets × Reps | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Incline Dumbbell Press** (30°) | 3–4 × 6–10 | Upper chest is the weak point on most naturals; DBs give a bigger stretch + free ROM. |
| **Flat DB Press or Weighted Dip** | 3 × 8–12 | Dips for a deep stretch at the bottom. Flat DB press if dips bother your shoulders. |

### SHOULDERS
| Movement | Sets × Reps | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Overhead Press** (barbell or DB, seated) | 3 × 6–10 | Front/overall delt mass and pressing strength. |
| **Lateral Raise** (DB or cable) | 4 × 12–20 | The most underrated move for the "3D" capped look. Recovers fast, do lots. |

### ARMS
| Movement | Sets × Reps | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Incline DB Curl** (biceps) | 3 × 8–12 | Arm behind the body = max long-head stretch = best biceps builder. |
| **Overhead Cable/DB Triceps Extension** | 3 × 10–15 | Long head is most of arm size; overhead position stretches it. |

### LEGS
| Movement | Sets × Reps | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Squat** (high-bar) or **Hack Squat** | 3–4 × 6–10 | Best overall quad + total leg mass. Hack squat if free squats wreck your back/balance. |
| **Romanian Deadlift** (RDL) | 3 × 8–12 | Best hamstring/glute builder — hinge with a deep stretch. |

### ABS
| Movement | Sets × Reps | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Weighted Cable Crunch / Hanging Leg Raise** | 3 × 10–15 | Abs are a muscle — they need load and progression, not 100 bodyweight crunches. |
| **Weighted Decline Crunch or Ab Wheel** | 3 × 8–15 | Train 2×/week. Visibility is mostly diet/body-fat, not crunch volume. |

## How to Progress (the part most people get wrong)

1. **Double progression** (primary lever): hit top of rep range on all sets → add
   2.5–5 lb → repeat.
2. **Log every set.** If it's not on paper, you're guessing. Beating your logbook = growth.
3. **Add a set** to a lagging muscle every ~2–3 weeks until near your volume ceiling, then hold.
4. **Deload every 6–10 weeks** (cut volume ~40–50% for a week) when joints ache or weights stall.
5. **Eat and sleep:** ~0.7–1 g protein/lb bodyweight, slight calorie surplus (~+200–300
   if bulking), 7–9h sleep. Recovery *is* where naturals make or break gains.

---

## TL;DR
- **Split:** Upper/Lower, 4×/week (each muscle 2×).
- **Volume:** 10–20 sets/muscle/week, build up gradually.
- **Intensity:** 0–3 reps from failure, every set.
- **Progress:** double progression + a logbook. Beat last week's numbers.
- **Naturals win on consistency and progressive overload over years — not on volume.**
