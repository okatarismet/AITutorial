# Set Numbers & Cardio Placement — Research Addendum

Companion to `natural-bodybuilding-research.md` and `workout-plan.md`. Two questions:
(1) how many sets, really — and (2) where does cardio go without killing gains.

---

## Part 1 — Set Numbers

The mistake most people make is tracking **one** number (weekly sets). There are
**two** that matter, and the per-*session* one is the one nobody talks about.

### The weekly budget: 10–20 sets/muscle/week
- **10–20 sets per muscle per week** is the high-ROI band. Start ~10–12 and build up.
- Below 10 underperforms; under ~5 sets/week is far worse than 10+.
- The 2025 dose-response data shows growth *keeps* rising past 20 sets for some trained
  lifters — but with steep diminishing returns and rising recovery cost. So 10–20 is the
  sweet spot, **not** a hard ceiling. More is "more, but less efficient and harder to recover."

### The per-session cap: ~6–8 effective sets/muscle (the part most people miss)
- Within a single session, effective sets **plateau hard at ~6–8 sets per muscle.** Past
  that you hit "junk volume": extra fatigue and recovery debt with little-to-no added growth.
- **Small muscles** (side delts, biceps, triceps): cap ~6–8 sets/session.
- **Large muscles** (back, quads, glutes): can productively take **10–12 sets/session.**
- This is *why* spreading volume works: **10 chest sets Mon + 10 Thu beats 20 in one day.**
  Your 2×/week Upper/Lower frequency keeps each session under the junk-volume cap while still
  hitting a high weekly total. Frequency isn't magic — it's how you bank weekly volume without
  any one session tipping into junk.

### Junk volume — the definition
Extra sets past the point of return. They don't add growth; they *do* add recovery demand,
which can eat into your *next* session's quality. More sets ≠ more gains once you're past
your per-session and weekly ceilings.

### The asterisk on all of it
Every number above assumes sets taken to **0–3 reps from failure (RIR).** Easy, far-from-
failure sets don't count toward the effective total. Volume only "counts" if the effort is there.

### How this maps to your current plan
Your `workout-plan.md` already lands inside these limits — no forced changes:

| Muscle | Per-session peak (your plan) | Session cap | Weekly total | Weekly band |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Chest | ~4 (Upper A/B) | 6–8 ✅ | ~11 | 10–20 ✅ |
| Back | ~7–8 (Upper B) | 10–12 ✅ | ~14 | 10–20 ✅ |

**per-session cap** — don't pile a 9th/10th set on a small muscle in one day. Add the set to
the *other* session that week instead (raise frequency/weekly total, not session density).

## Part 2 — Where to Add Cardio

The "cardio kills gains" interference effect is **real but manageable** — and overstated at
moderate doses. It bites only under specific conditions. Control three levers: **type, timing,
and separation from legs.**

### Lever 1 — Type
- **LISS / Zone 2** (incline walk, cycling, elliptical, light row): negligible interference.
  This is your default. 70–80% of max HR, conversational pace.
- **HIIT:** keep it rare. High fatigue that competes directly with leg recovery and pressing
  power. Optional, not foundational.

### Lever 2 — Timing
- **Separate cardio from lifting by 4+ hours** when you can. Cardio activates AMPK, which
  blunts the mTOR signaling that drives muscle growth; the gap lets that settle.
- **If same session: lift first, cardio after.** Never gas yourself with cardio before heavy work.
- **Worst case (avoid):** hard cardio *before* lifting, OR hard *lower-body* cardio (running,
  sprints) on/near leg days. The big ~50% growth hit in studies showed up when intense cardio
  immediately followed same-region training.

### Lever 3 — Separation from legs
- Keep intense or **impact** cardio **away from Tue (Lower A) and Fri (Lower B).**
- Prefer **low-impact** modalities (incline walk, bike, elliptical) to spare the legs for squats/RDLs.

### Placement in your Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri split

```
Mon  Upper A   → optional LISS after (or PM), 20–30 min
Tue  Lower A   → steps only; no hard/impact cardio
Wed  REST      → best LISS slot, 30–40 min Zone 2
Thu  Upper B   → optional LISS after (or PM), 20–30 min
Fri  Lower B   → steps only; no hard/impact cardio
Sat  REST      → optional 2nd LISS slot
Sun  REST      → optional LISS / long easy walk
```

**Priority order:** rest days (Wed/Sat/Sun) > after Upper days (Mon/Thu) > never stacked on Lower days.

### Dose
- **~60–150 min Zone 2 per week** (2–3 sessions × 20–40 min) covers health + conditioning
  without denting gains.
- **2× 30 min beats 1× 60 min** — split it up.
- **Daily 8–10k steps** is the cheapest, zero-interference baseline — do this first, add
  structured cardio second.

### Don't forget
Cardio burns calories. **Eat them back** — keep the slight surplus (+200–300 kcal if bulking)
and protein (~0.7–1 g/lb) the plan already calls for, or you'll stall growth via the back door.

---

## TL;DR
- **Sets:** 10–20/muscle/**week**, AND ≤6–8/muscle/**session** for small muscles (10–12 for big
  ones). Spread volume across your 2 weekly sessions — don't cram. Everything counts only near failure.
- **Junk volume** = sets past those caps: recovery cost, no growth.
- **Cardio:** default to LISS/Zone 2; put it on rest days or after Upper days; keep impact cardio
  off leg days; 2–3× 20–40 min/week; 4h+ from lifting when possible; eat back the burn.
